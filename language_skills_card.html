Keep working on your:

Vocabulary

The range of words you know and are able to use accurately. A strong vocabulary helps you sound precise and professional rather than vague or repetitive.

Grammar

The rules of the language — sentence structure, verb tenses, articles, and word order. Correct grammar ensures your meaning is clear and creates a strong professional impression.

Fluency

Expressing yourself smoothly and confidently, without long pauses or frequent self-correction. Fluent speakers sound natural even when they make small mistakes.

Coherence

Organizing your ideas in a clear, logical way so the listener can easily follow your point. Good coherence means using connectors and staying on topic without jumping around.

Pronunciation

How clearly and accurately you produce the sounds of English. Good pronunciation means listeners understand you easily — even if you have an accent, clarity always matters more than sounding like a native speaker.
