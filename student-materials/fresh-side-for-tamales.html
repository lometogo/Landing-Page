Jicama⁹ salad, with lime juice and chili powder or tajín, cilantro if desired, salt and sugar to taste. If I want more sweet, orange supremes¹⁰ in there (or whatever citrus you like), if I want more savory, sliced red cabbage (or whatever cabbage you've got).

Julienne¹¹ the jicama and mix everything ahead of time, tastes better after it sits for a while, lasts a couple days. The spicy carrot situation is also the correct answer imo, and any kind of fresh radish preparation you like — I once had radish quarters dipped in leftover elote sauce and it was awesome.

I love finely diced avocado, jicama, corn, onions/shallots, cilantro, and chili lime oil. The crunch from the jicama and corn is really nice paired with the creamy smoothness of the avocado and tamale filling. Definitely let the mixture rest in the fridge so the chili lime oil marinates. Mmmmmm.

Jicama: (HEE-kah-mah) — a crunchy, mildly sweet root vegetable native to Mexico, often eaten raw in salads or as a snack with chili and lime; Mexican turnip, yam bean. The jicama added a satisfying crunch to the salad without overpowering the other flavors. ↑ back
